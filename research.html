Most recent publication

In the publication below, we study the previously unexplored influence of sex allocation on the emergence of cooperative breeding. First, we use a population dynamic model to determine the evolutionarily stable sex allocation strategies in a selfish population. Second, we use inclusive fitness arguments to determine how an auxiliary can allocate resources to give the greatest evolutionary incentive to helping. We found the optimal helper allocation strategy was to distribute resources equally among the sexes. This is due to a breeder's strategy equating the marginal gains from allocating in each sex.

Dunn, J. D., Vujicic, T., Wild, G. (2015). Sex allocation and the emergence of helping in cooperatively breeding species. Theoretical Population Biology, 104:1-9.
